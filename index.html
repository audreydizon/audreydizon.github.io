Family

My family is a huge aspect of my life. I grew up with three brothers; Nakoa, Hayden and Kona. They have always fueled my determination to achieve my goals and pushed me to be my best self. In being guided through life by two older brothers, a sense of grit is instilled in how I approach obstacles and I've learned the importance of standing firm on who I am. Furthermore, being a big sister has fostered my ability to be both a leading and loving presence for my younger brother. In regards to my wonderful parents, nothing short of gratification is felt for the countless lessons they've taught my siblings and me, and all the sacrifices they've made for us.

Hometown Best Friends

My hometown best friends have truly shaped me into who I am and taught me the importance of life-long relationships built on loyalty and trust. I grew up in a tight-nit neighborhood community, so 3/4 of my best friends live a few steps away from me and our parents pretty much raised us all as a family. Amanda, Lauryn, Daniella, and Siena have continued to be constants in my life, and I'll forever be grateful for all of the memories we share.

College Teammates

Throughout my time in college, my soccer teammates have been the best group of girls I could've asked to be around. In the crazy world of college athletics, there is so much change, hardship, wins, and losses, and we've been through it all together. Being surrounded by such a diverse group of personalities and backgrounds, I've learned something new from each of them that I'll carry with me post-college. I spend the majority of every day around them, whether its weightlifting, conditioning, or just hanging out, and I wouldn't trade it for anything.

Soccer

I've been playing soccer since I was five years old. Throughout the 15 years of playing the sport, it has always been a central part of my life and identity. Soccer has taught me lessons that are applicable to every field of my life, pushed me to go past what I thought were my limits, and brought me life-long relationships like no other. It has always been my competitive outlet, and although it has its ups and downs, it has contributed so much to my personal growth and given me opportunities I dreamed of as a little girl. I now play soccer here at Gonzaga, and it is been the most impactful experience and a blessing beyond imaginable.

Zaga

My journey at Gonzaga has been such a pivotal time of my life. While here, I've gone through my greatest challenges, but also achieved my greatest accomplishments, bettering myself as a person, student, and athlete along the way. To be able to further my education is a blessing in itself, but to do it at a place that values a wholistic experience and competes at the highest level athletically, makes it so much more of a privelidge. I'm beyond thankful for my time as a Zag so far!

Roots

My Filipina heritage is an essential part of my identity. My Dad immigrated from the Philippines to the United States when he was young. Through his journey, I've gained a perspective on life centered around gratitude, resilience, and the importance of family. Being Filipina has instilled a sense of grit in how I approach challenges and deepened my determination to succeed in environments where my ethnicity is often underrepresented. I carry my Filipina roots with pride and strive to always live in a way that honors the sacrifices my family has made throughout their journey.
